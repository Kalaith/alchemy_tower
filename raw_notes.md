The best version of the memory-fragment idea

I would not make them full literal flashbacks every time. That can get stiff and game-y fast.

Instead, make them feel like echoes bound to place:

when you restore the greenhouse, you glimpse the wizard testing ways to restore pollination and soil vitality

when you restore containment, you witness the moment care drifted into control

when you restore the rune workshop, you see the scale of the valley-wide distribution system

archives show the wizard trying to justify the risk

observatory reveals the final choice and failure

So each floor answers two questions:

What was this room meant to do for the valley?

What part of the wizard does it reveal?

That’s very strong.

The critical issue to watch

The biggest trap is making the fragments into lore delivery instead of gameplay fuel.

If a fragment is just:

watch scene

learn backstory

leave

then it becomes decorative parchment.

Each fragment should instead do three jobs:

reveal character

explain the floor’s ecological purpose

point the player toward a present-day repair

So the fragment should end with something actionable:

restore pollinator support

calm a disrupted species

recalibrate distribution runes

reconstruct a missing archive record

choose how to complete the final restoration

That way the memory is not a museum plaque. It is a key.

The second critical issue: don’t make the wizard too correct

If the fragments all say “the wizard knew exactly what to do, but tragedy happened,” the story gets a bit polished and bloodless.

You want some crackle:

good intentions

real insight

growing pressure

mounting overconfidence

rationalizations

regret

Let the player see both brilliance and error. Otherwise the wizard becomes a sainted tutorial bird with paperwork.

The third critical issue: use the delivery method your game can actually support

This one matters a lot.

Your current implementation already has archives, milestones, journal surfaces, overlays, progression gates, authored NPC dialogue, and a triggered ending, but it does not currently show support for cutscenes or dialogue trees.

So I would avoid building these fragments as ambitious cinematic sequences.
Build them in an engine-friendly way:

room-entry echo text

short visualized memory overlays

archive reconstructions

ghostly afterimages in a room

crow dialogue that changes immediately after a fragment

journal updates that reinterpret what you just saw

That keeps scope sane and matches what you already have. The plan itself says story should be content implementation, not new engine work, and to avoid adding many new systems just to tell the story.
